# Assessment: AI for Climate — From Technological Solutions to Relational Accountability (Stein 2026)

**Not a technology — a critique of the field, and a sharpener for our Responsible-AI lens.** The rubric's
job here is to recognize that this is *not* an AI innovation to score, but a perspective that should
**reshape how we apply lens 6 and the Responsible-AI dimension to every other assessment.**

## What it is
A single-author **Comment** (npj Climate Action) by **Sharon Stein** (UBC, Musqueam Territory). Argues
that dominant "AI-for-climate" reproduces three cultural patterns that drive ecological breakdown —
**human exceptionalism, technosolutionism/optimization, epistemic universalism** — and invites
"**relational accountability**": AI accountable to diverse human communities, species, and generations.
Not anti-AI; conceptual (no datasets).

## Verdict
**Important perspective (not a technology assessment).** The tool correctly routes this *away* from the
innovation lenses and *into* the Responsible-AI / justice dimension. Treat its argument as a standing input
that should temper any assessment relying on top-down optimization of ecosystems or data drawn from
Indigenous lands. *In a fuller ontology this would live as a `Thesis`/perspective, not a technology Assessment.*
