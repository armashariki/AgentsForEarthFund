# Hot Science Agent Criteria - Climate Change and Global Warming Focus (April 2026)

## Search Focus

Generate a list of articles and underlying studies first published in April 2026 that directly address climate change, global warming, or closely related climate impacts. Include articles surfaced through ScienceDaily when they trace back to an eligible underlying peer-reviewed paper or official institutional report.

## Target Month

The target month is April 2026.

Only include an item in the final top-candidate set when the underlying peer-reviewed journal article, official report, attribution report, or institutional data release was first published, including online-first publication, between April 1, 2026 and April 30, 2026.

Popular press coverage, including ScienceDaily articles, may be published after the underlying peer-reviewed article, as long as the underlying publication itself is within April 2026.

## Core Inclusion Criteria

### Topical Relevance

The study must directly address climate change, global warming, or climate impacts.

Eligible domains include:

- Physical climate science
- Extreme heat
- Drought
- Extreme weather
- Wildfire risk
- Flooding, including Amazon flooding
- Sea level rise
- Oceans and ocean warming
- Cryosphere, glaciers, ice sheets, sea ice, permafrost
- Ecosystems and climate-change interactions
- Human health and climate-related pollutants
- Agriculture and food systems
- Economics of climate impacts
- Adaptation or mitigation when the item reports climate-relevant impacts, risks, or evidence rather than only evaluating an intervention

### Source Credibility

Eligible sources are:

- Peer-reviewed journal articles
- Official institutional reports or data releases
- Formally published attribution reports, including World Weather Attribution
- Institutional data releases from sources such as WMO, NSIDC, Copernicus, or NOAA

Prefer items with credible author affiliations, including universities, research institutes, agencies, or recognized scientific organizations.

### Publication Timing

The original source page must confirm the publication date.

The qualifying date is the underlying study/report/data release date, not the press article date.

Use the canonical online-first publication date when available.

### Research Significance

Prioritize items with at least one of the following:

- Novel findings
- Large or important datasets
- Clear climate-impact implications
- Clear climate-risk implications
- Clear relevance to policy, public risk, or scientific understanding
- Methodological advances only when they produce or support substantive climate findings beyond simply improving the method

### Discoverability Signals

Prioritize items with one or more of the following signals:

- Covered by ScienceDaily
- Covered by EurekAlert
- Covered by major media
- Published in high-impact or well-known journals
- Highlighted by editors, featured collections, or institutional science communications

## Verification Requirements

Each included item must trace to an original paper, report, or official data release.

Each included item should include, where available:

- DOI or official source page
- Original title
- Authors
- Journal or issuing institution
- Publication date
- Link to ScienceDaily or other press coverage when available

Media coverage must accurately reflect the underlying study.

## De-Duplication Rule

Return one entry per underlying study, report, or dataset.

Group all press coverage under the single underlying entry.

Do not create separate entries for ScienceDaily, EurekAlert, institutional press releases, repository records, or media summaries when they describe the same underlying paper/report/dataset.

## Eligible Categories

Eligible final categories are:

- Peer-reviewed papers
- Formally published attribution reports
- Institutional data releases

Preprints may be surfaced separately only if the pipeline supports a preprint bucket, but they should not be mixed with peer-reviewed final candidates.

## Exclusion Rules

Exclude:

- Items outside April 2026 based on the underlying publication date
- Unverified media-only claims
- Duplicate entries
- Wikipedia or non-authoritative sources
- Articles evaluating or proposing interventions or solutions without a substantive climate-impact finding
- Papers focused only on intervention performance, such as efficacy of new photovoltaics or lowering electric vehicle costs
- Pure methods papers where the only output is improved methodology, sampling technique, retrieval method, or modeling approach
- Items without a direct lens on climate change, global warming, or closely related climate impacts

## Output Expectations

For each final candidate, provide:

- Why it qualifies
- The underlying publication date and date source
- The source database/feed/API that found it
- The original DOI or official source link
- Any ScienceDaily or other press coverage
- The climate-impact domain tags
- Any uncertainty or manual-review caveat
